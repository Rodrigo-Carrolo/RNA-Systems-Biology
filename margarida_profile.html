Academic Background

Margarida Gama-Carvalho is an Assistant Professor at the Chemistry and Biochemistry Department of the Faculty of Sciences, University of Lisbon, where she serves as the Coordinator of the MSc program in Biochemistry and Biomedicine.

She holds a PhD in Biomedical Sciences from the Faculty of Medicine, University of Lisbon (2003), and has been involved in undergraduate and post-graduate teaching for more than 25 years. Her academic journey began with a Biology degree from the Faculty of Sciences, University of Lisbon (1994), followed by Pedagogical Aptitude and Scientific Capacity examinations in Molecular and Cellular Biology at the Faculty of Medicine of Lisbon (2000).

Research Focus

Her research focuses on understanding the molecular mechanisms that control gene expression at the RNA level, with particular emphasis on post-transcriptional regulation, RNA-protein interactions, and the role of RNA networks in cellular function and disease.

The laboratory brings together computational and experimental approaches to characterize and model regulatory networks involved in the coordination of gene expression programs in vertebrates, with special interest in regulatory events occurring at the post-transcriptional level.

She is also a member of the executive board of BioISI and serves as the Principal Investigator of the Gene Expression and Regulation Group.
